# Beaver Dam Analogue (BDA) Design Guide

## Introduction
Beaver Dam Analogues (BDAs) are human-built structures that mimic natural beaver dams. They are used to restore stream functions, enhance habitat, and improve water retention in degraded river systems. This guide outlines key design considerations for constructing effective BDAs.

## Site Selection & Placement
- **Stream Width**: BDAs are most effective in streams 1–10 meters wide. Wider streams may require multiple structures in series.
- **Channel Gradient**: Optimal gradient range is 0.5–3%. Steeper gradients increase shear stress and may require more robust anchoring.
- **Valley Morphology**: Choose locations with well-defined banks and floodplain connectivity to maximize wetland creation.
- **Existing Vegetation**: Presence of willow, alder, or other riparian plants provides natural reinforcement and future food for beavers.
- **Land Ownership & Permits**: Ensure proper permissions and compliance with local, state, and federal regulations.

## Materials
### Primary Structural Elements
- **Wood Posts**: Pressure-treated or naturally rot‑resistant wood (e.g., cedar, juniper) 10–15 cm diameter, driven 1–1.5 m into the substrate.
- **Willow Stakes**: Live willow cuttings (2–3 cm diameter, 1–2 m long) for live staking; they root and provide long‑term stability.
- **Rock & Gravel**: Used to fill interstitial spaces and add mass. Ideal diameter 10–30 cm.

### Fasteners & Auxiliary Items
- **Galvanized Wire** (12‑gauge) to lash posts together.
- **Wooden Stakes** for temporary bracing during construction.
- **Geotextile Fabric** (optional) to prevent sediment wash‑out in highly erosive settings.

## Structural Design
### Single‑Row Post BDA
Simplest design for small streams (< 3 m wide).
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│             Stream Flow →                   │
│  POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST    │
│  └──────────────────────────────────────┘   │
│        Willow stakes & brush packed         │
│        between posts                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
**Specifications**:
- Post spacing: 30–50 cm
- Post embedment: ≥ 1 m below channel bottom
- Height: 30–80 cm above summer base flow

### Multi‑Row BDA
For wider streams or higher hydrologic energy.
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST    │
│  POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST    │
│  POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST─POST    │
│  (Rows spaced 0.5–1 m apart)                │
│  Brush/rock fill between rows               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
**Specifications**:
- 2–4 rows staggered
- Row spacing 0.5–1 m
- Fill material: locally sourced woody debris, rock, and live cuttings

## Design Considerations by Stream Width
| Stream Width (m) | Recommended Design          | Post Diameter (cm) | Notes                              |
|------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------------|
| 1–3              | Single‑row, brush‑filled    | 10–12              | Suitable for low‑gradient reaches  |
| 3–6              | Double‑row, rock‑filled     | 12–15              | Add rock toe for scour protection  |
| 6–10             | Triple‑row, hybrid fill     | 15–18              | Consider foundation logs upstream  |
